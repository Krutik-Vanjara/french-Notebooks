## Your Comprehensive French Exam Study Guide

### I. Mastering Verb Tenses: The Storyteller’s Tools

The sources focus heavily on controlling time, which is crucial for narration and giving instructions.

| Concept | Layman Explanation | How to Use in Real Life (Sentence Construction) | Source(s) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Le Présent (Present Tense)** | Used for actions happening right now, things you do regularly (habits), and universal truths. | **Habit:** *Le matin, je bois du café.* (In the morning, I drink coffee). **Action Now:** *Je regarde un film.* (I am watching a film). | |
| **L'Impératif (Commands)** | Used to **give orders or advice**. It only exists for *tu*, *nous*, and *vous* forms. | **Order (Tu):** *Joue !* (Play!). **Negative Order (Vous):** *Ne jouez pas !* (Don't play!). **Advice (Pronominal):** *Promène-toi !* (Go for a walk!). | |
| **Le Passé Composé (PC)** | **The Snapshot Past:** Used for a **single, specific action terminated in the past**. | **Event:** *Sur un coup de tête, j’ai acheté un billet d’avion.* (On a whim, I bought a plane ticket). **Formation:** Aux. *Avoir/Être* (in present) + Past Participle. **P.P. Irregulars:** *eu* (avoir), *pris* (prendre), *vu* (voir). | |
| **L'Imparfait (Imperfect)** | **The Background Past:** Used for **descriptions** (setting the scene), **ongoing/continuous actions**, and **past habits or memories**. | **Description/Context:** *Il faisait gris à Paris. Je portais une robe blanche.* (It was grey in Paris. I was wearing a white dress). **Habit:** *Quand j’étais enfant, je chantais dans une chorale.* (When I was a child, I used to sing in a choir). | |
| **Le Plus-que-Parfait (PQP)** | **The Earlier Past:** Used to describe an action that **happened *before* another action in the past** (like the English "had done"). | **Usage:** *J’avais mis de l’argent de côté* (I **had put** money aside). **Formation:** Aux. *Être/Avoir* conjugated in the **Imparfait** + Past Participle. | |
| **Le Futur Simple** | Used for a future event, a **project, a prediction, a promise, or an order**. | **Prediction:** *En 2060, les voitures voleront.* (In 2060, cars will fly). **Order:** *Vous ferez tous les exercices pour demain.* (You will do all the exercises for tomorrow). | |
| **Le Futur Proche** | Used for an event that will happen in the **near future** or an upcoming change. | **Near Future:** *Demain soir, nous allons dîner chez Carole.* (Tomorrow evening, we are going to dine at Carole’s). **Formation:** *Aller* (present) + Infinitive. | |

### II. Nouns, Adjectives, and Agreement

These rules govern how you modify and refer to things in French.

#### A. Articles and Determinants
The determinant precedes a noun and agrees in gender and number.

| Concept | Layman Explanation | How to Use in Real Life (Sentence Construction) | Source(s) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Articles Définis** | Introduce a **specific** noun (unique, identified, or general concept). (le, la, l', les). | *Je vais visiter **le** musée d’Orsay.* (A specific, identified place). | |
| **Articles Indéfinis** | Introduce a noun that is **not specific** or represents a category. (un, une, des). | *Je voudrais **une** rose.* (A single, non-specific item). | |
| **Articles Partitifs** | Indicate an **undetermined quantity** or non-countable nouns (like liquids or abstract concepts). (du, de la, de l', des). | *Tu as **du** sel ?* (Do you have [some] salt?). | |
| **Contracted Articles** | Articles *le* and *les* must merge with the prepositions *à* and *de*. *À* + *le* = **au**; *À* + *les* = **aux**. *DE* + *le* = **du**; *DE* + *les* = **des**. | *Il pense **aux** films qu’il a vus.* (He thinks about the films he saw). *Il parle **des** films qu’il a vus.* (He talks about the films he saw). | |
| **Possessive/Demonstrative Adjectives** | **Possessive** indicates ownership (mon, ma, mes). **Demonstrative** points out a specific thing (ce, cet, cette, ces). | *C’est **ma** voiture.* (It is my car). *Tu as vu **cette** photo ?* (Did you see this photo?). | |

#### B. Adjective Agreement
Adjectives must agree in gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular/plural) with the noun they modify.

*   **Standard Rule:** Add **-e** for feminine singular (*grand* $\rightarrow$ *grande*) and **-s** for plural (*grands*).
*   **Adjectives ending in -e:** Remain the same in the masculine and feminine singular (e.g., *calme*).
*   **Doubling Consonants:** Adjectives ending in *-l*, *-s*, or *-n* often double the consonant before adding *-e* (e.g., *bon* $\rightarrow$ *bonne*; *naturel* $\rightarrow$ *naturelle*).
*   **Irregular Endings:**
    *   *-if* becomes *-ive* (*sportif* $\rightarrow$ *sportive*).
    *   *-eux* or *-eur* becomes *-euse* (*dangereux* $\rightarrow$ *dangereuse*; *menteur* $\rightarrow$ *menteuse*).
    *   *-c* changes to *-que* (*public* $\rightarrow$ *publique*).
    *   Specific Irregulars: *blanc* $\rightarrow$ *blanche*; *beau* $\rightarrow$ *belle*.

#### C. Pronouns
Pronouns replace nouns to avoid repetition.

*   **COD (Direct Object):** Replaces a person or thing that is the direct object (who/what is verbed). Forms: *le, la, l', les, me, te, nous, vous*.
*   **COI (Indirect Object):** Replaces mostly **animate** nouns (people/animals) introduced by the preposition *à* (answering "to whom?"). Forms: *lui* (to him/her), *leur* (to them), *me, te, nous, vous*.
    *   **Usage:** *Je **lui** explique mon problème.* (I explain my problem **to him**).
*   **Pronoun Y (Place/Inanimate À):** Replaces a **place** or an **inanimate** object introduced by *à* (meaning "there").
    *   **Usage:** *Ils **y** vont chaque année.* (They go **there** every year).
*   **Pronoun EN (Quantity/De):** Replaces nouns introduced by *de*, partitive articles, indefinite articles, or numbers (related to quantity).
    *   **Usage:** *Tu **en** as encore besoin ?* (Do you need **some** more?).
*   **Relative Pronouns:** Connect two clauses: *qui* (subject), *que* (direct object), *où* (place/time), *dont* (related to *de*).
    *   **Usage:** *J’aime le pull **que** tu as acheté.* (I like the sweater **that** you bought).

### III. Practical Tools for Communication

These concepts help you structure information about time, location, and social interaction.

#### A. Time Expressions (Les expressions temporelles)

These tools precisely place actions in time.

| Expression | Function | Real-Life Application | Source(s) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Il y a + durée** | Indicates a specific moment **in the past**. | *J’ai acheté cet appartement **il y a deux ans**.* (I bought this apartment **two years ago**). | |
| **Depuis + durée/date** | Indicates a situation that **started in the past and continues today**. | *J’habite à Bruxelles **depuis deux ans**.* (I have lived in Brussels **for two years**). | |
| **Dans + durée** | Indicates an action that will occur **in the future** after the period indicated. | *Je vais déménager **dans trois semaines**.* (I am going to move **in three weeks**). | |
| **Pendant + durée** | Indicates a **period of time** (often terminated). | *J'ai dormi **pendant huit heures**.* | |
| **Pour + durée** | Indicates a **planned duration**. | *Le musée... portera le nom de «!Musée du Louvre » **pour 50 ans**.* (The museum will carry the name... for 50 years). | |
| **En + durée** | Indicates the **duration necessary** to complete an action. | *J'ai lu le livre **en deux jours**.* | |
| **Avant de + INFINITIF** | Indicates that the action in the infinitive happens **AFTER** the action described next. (The main action happens *before* the infinitive). | *Tu dois manger **avant de partir**.* (You must eat before leaving). | |
| **Après + Être/Avoir à l'infinitif + participe passé** | Indicates that the action in the infinitive happened **BEFORE** the action described next. | ***Après avoir été** un château, le Louvre est devenu un musée.* (After having been a castle, the Louvre became a museum). | |

#### B. Prepositions of Place (Cities and Countries)

Prepositions change depending on the gender and number of the country or city.

| Destination / Action (Going to/Living in) | Origin (Coming from) | Examples | Source(s) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **AU** (Masculine Country) | **DU** (Masculine Country) | *Je vais **au** Sénégal.* / *Elle vient **du** Japon.*. | |
| **EN** (Feminine Country or Vowel) | **DE** (Feminine Country) or **D’** (Vowel) | *Nous travaillons **en** France.* / *Il vient **d’** Inde.*. | |
| **AUX** (Plural Country) | **DES** (Plural Country) | *J’habite **aux** États-Unis.* / *Je viens **des** Philippines.*. | |
| **À** (City) | **DE / D’** (City) | *J’étudie **à** Londres.* / *Je viens **de** Barcelone.*. | |

#### C. Communicative Functions

The sources provide vocabulary and exercises related to:

*   **Giving Orders and Advice:** Using the Imperative (*Joue!, Ne te promène pas !*).
*   **Recounting Past Events:** Using PC and Imparfait to narrate a story.
*   **Offering and Thanking:** *Offrir, remercier*.
*   **Inviting and Apologizing:** *Inviter, proposer, accepter, refuser, s’excuser*.
*   **Describing Geography and Cities:** Referring to French rivers (La Loire, La Seine, Le Rhin), or urban features (rive droite/gauche, quai, monument, boulangerie, bureau de tabac, station de métro).

### IV. Vocabulaire and Cultural Knowledge

Your exam may test your general comprehension of French culture and urban life.

*   **Parisian Life:** Know terms related to the neighborhood (*Le quartier*). The city is divided into **arrondissements**. Vocabulary includes: *une rive* (bank), *un quai* (embankment), *un citadin* (city dweller), *bobo* (bourgeois bohemian), *piéton* (pedestrian).
*   **Rivers of France:** La Loire is the longest river (1020km). La Seine runs through Paris and flows into the English Channel (La Manche) at Le Havre. Le Rhin serves as a natural border between France and Germany.
*   **Understanding Abbreviations:** French speakers use abbreviations extensively to save time.
    *   *la télé* (la télévision); *l'ordi* (l’ordinateur); *le prof* (le professeur).
    *   *c'est sympa* (c'est sympathique); *c'est perso* (c'est personnel).

### V. Teacher’s Advice and Exam Strategy

**1. Know Your Tense Distinctions:** This is the most complex grammar point. If you need to tell a story:
*   Use the **Imparfait** for the **context** (what was happening, the weather, emotions: *Il faisait froid, j’étais fâché...*).
*   Use the **Passé Composé** for the **actions that moved the story forward** (the plot points: *Je suis rentré, j’ai croisé un ami*).
*   Use the **Plus-que-parfait** only if you need to refer to something that happened *even earlier* than the rest of the story (*j’avais mis* l’argent de côté avant de partir).

**2. Watch for Agreement:** When you write sentences using the *Passé Composé* with **ÊTRE** (14 verbs like *aller*, *venir*, *naître*, plus pronominal verbs), the Past Participle **must agree** with the subject.
*   *Elle est allé**e*** (Feminine subject).
*   *Ils se sont promen**és*** (Plural subject, pronominal verb).

**3. Practice Commands (Impératif):** Remember the formation is derived from the Present tense, but you **drop the ‘s’** for *-ER* verbs in the *tu* form (e.g., *Tu joues* $\rightarrow$ *Joue !*), unless followed by *y* or *en*.

**4. Master Key Vocabulary for Instructions:** Familiarize yourself with exam prompt keywords, as they tell you exactly what to do:
*   ***Décrire/Faire une description***: Use the Imparfait and lots of adjectives.
*   ***Rédiger***: Write.
*   ***Compléter***: Fill in, complete (often conjugating verbs or selecting pronouns).
*   ***Associer/Relier***: Match, link.
*   ***Quel est l’intrus***: Find the odd one out.
